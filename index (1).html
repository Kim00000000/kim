Huricans and Storms

Location: Hurricane is a storm that happens in the Atlantic Ocean and northeastern Pacific Ocean while Typhoon happns in the northwestern Pacific Ocean.

Occurring Date: Hurricane occurs between August to November but occurring dates of Typhoon are concentrated in May to October.

Meaning and Origin of words: The word hurricane comes from the Taino Native American word, hurucane, meaning evil spirit of the wind. the word typhoon comes from Chinese which means giant wind.

Dangers: There are plenty of dangers which Hurricanes and Typhoons cause but the most common and dangerous effects are Storm Surge and Storm Tide. Storm Surge is an abnormal rise of water generated by a storm's winds. Storm surge can reach heights well over 20 feet and can span hundreds of miles of coastline. Storm Tide is the water level rise during a storm due to the combination of storm surge and the astronomical tide. These can result in loss of life, buildings destroyed, beach and dune erosion and road and bridge damage along the coast.

Good effects: Hurricanes and Typhoons can cause Bacteria and red tide breakup and help to balance global heat. These also replenish of barrier islands and inland plant life, spread plant seeds.
